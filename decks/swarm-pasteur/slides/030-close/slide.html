What Good Looks Like

A useful incident deck ends with a checklist, not a slogan

1

Detect quickly

What broke, who is affected, and who owns the response in the next ten minutes?

2

Contain decisively

What can we stop, roll back, mute, or isolate right now to reduce user impact?

3

Communicate regularly

What is our next update, when will it land, and who needs it first?

Core Reminder

Discipline beats drama.

Teams look prepared when they repeat the same calm loop under pressure: detect, contain, communicate, then return to normal with a clear postmortem.

Use the same three-move sequence in status updates, incident drills, and post-incident reviews so the response model becomes automatic.
