Closing Operating Model

Think of the night port as six linked layers that leaders have to keep in sync

1. Berth and cranes

Protect vessel sequence and equipment uptime.

2. Yard buffer

Maintain usable density and retrieval logic.

3. Gate and inland flow

Keep truck and rail exits predictable.

4. Regulatory and data signal

Make release, customs, and status data match reality.

5. Labor and safety

Keep the shift staffed, visible, and disciplined.

6. Utilities and recovery

Treat power, comms, maintenance, and incident response as core capacity.

If leaders can see these six layers together, they can manage the night shift as a system instead of reacting to isolated delays.
