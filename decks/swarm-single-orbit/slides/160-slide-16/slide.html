Bottlenecks

Night operations usually stall at the boundaries between systems, not inside one machine

Common overnight bottlenecks

  • Boxes physically available but still missing release status.
  • Truck appointments arriving faster than yard retrieval capacity.
  • Rail cut-offs compressing yard work into one narrow window.
  • Equipment downtime that shifts queues from berth to gate.

What makes them dangerous

These bottlenecks do not stay local. They bounce between systems, so the delay is often diagnosed one layer too late.

Boundary failures create the most management work because no single team owns them completely.
