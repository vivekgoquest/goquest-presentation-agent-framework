Maintenance

Overnight maintenance protects tomorrow’s throughput, but it has to coexist with live operations

Planned windows

Teams use the lower-demand pockets of the night for inspections, fueling, and targeted equipment work.

Deferred risk

Skipping night maintenance can look efficient, then show up as crane downtime or gate friction the next day.

Coordination need

Maintenance only works when planning and operations agree on what can be taken offline and when.

For leaders, maintenance is part of the operating model, not a support afterthought. The port needs planned capacity loss to avoid unplanned capacity collapse.
