Safety Layer

At night, safety is the command system that keeps speed from turning into risk

Traffic separation

Pedestrian paths, truck lanes, and equipment corridors must stay distinct even during surge periods.

Visibility discipline

Lighting, spotter routines, and radio clarity matter more when operators have fewer visual cues.

Stop-work authority

Night teams need explicit authority to pause operations when weather, fatigue, or equipment risk rises.

Leaders should think of safety controls as throughput protection. They reduce the kind of incident that freezes multiple flows at once.
