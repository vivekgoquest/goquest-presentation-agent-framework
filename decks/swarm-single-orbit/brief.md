# Swarm Single Orbit Brief

## Goal

Create a 20-slide presentation that explains how a port operates through the night shift, with a focus on the invisible handoffs that keep cargo moving while the rest of the city sleeps.

## Audience

City logistics leaders, terminal operators, infrastructure managers, and public-sector stakeholders responsible for freight reliability.

## Tone

Clear, operational, presentation-ready, and lightly documentary rather than promotional.

## Must Include

- freight handoffs across vessel, yard, truck, and rail
- customs and documentation flow
- trucking and gate operations
- utility systems that keep the port live at night
- labor shifts and role changes
- safety controls and command structure
- data flow across terminal, customs, and carrier systems
- a closing operating model that leaders can use to reason about the night shift

## Optional Inputs

- none yet

## Slide Goals

- Slides 1-5: frame the night-shift system and the first physical handoffs.
- Slides 6-10: explain the yard, trucking, rail, utilities, and labor layer.
- Slides 11-15: explain safety, data, control, maintenance, and security.
- Slides 16-20: explain bottlenecks, city impact, operator metrics, cascading failures, and the closing operating model.

## Constraints

- keep the deck to exactly 20 slides
- keep language operational and useful for leaders, not cinematic
- stay inside the deck workspace and use no cross-workspace assets
- keep each slide readable in preview, screenshots, PDF, and finalize output
